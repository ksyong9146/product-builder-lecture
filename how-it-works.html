How Animal Face Test Works

A deep dive into our AI-powered facial analysis technology

The Three-Step Process

Taking the Animal Face Test is simple, but the technology behind it is sophisticated. Here's what happens when you upload your photo:

Step 1: Image Upload

When you select a photo, it's loaded directly into your browser's memory. The image is converted into a format that our AI model can process - specifically, a grid of pixel values representing the colors and brightness at each point in the image.

Important: Your image never leaves your device. All processing happens locally in your web browser using JavaScript.

Step 2: AI Analysis

Our Convolutional Neural Network processes your image through multiple layers of analysis. Each layer detects increasingly complex features - from simple edges and shapes to facial structures and expressions. The model compares these features against patterns it learned during training.

Step 3: Result Generation

The model outputs a probability score for each category (dog-like and cat-like). The category with the higher probability becomes your result, along with a percentage indicating the confidence level. You'll also receive a personalized description based on your result.

Understanding Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

At the heart of Animal Face Test is a Convolutional Neural Network, one of the most powerful technologies in modern artificial intelligence. Let's break down how it works:

What is a Neural Network?

A neural network is a computing system inspired by the biological neural networks in our brains. It consists of interconnected nodes (artificial neurons) organized in layers. Each connection has a weight that adjusts during training, allowing the network to learn patterns from data.

What Makes CNNs Special?

Convolutional Neural Networks are specifically designed for processing visual data. They use a technique called "convolution" to scan images with small filters, detecting features at different positions. This makes them excellent at recognizing patterns regardless of where they appear in an image.

The Layers of Our CNN

Input Layer

Receives the raw pixel values from your uploaded image. The image is typically resized to a standard dimension (e.g., 224x224 pixels) to ensure consistent processing.

Convolutional Layers

Apply filters that detect specific features like edges, curves, and textures. Early layers detect simple features; deeper layers combine these into complex patterns like eyes, noses, and facial shapes.

Pooling Layers

Reduce the spatial dimensions of the data while retaining important information. This makes the network more efficient and helps it focus on the most relevant features.

Fully Connected Layers

Take all the detected features and combine them to make a final classification decision. These layers learn which combinations of features indicate dog-like vs. cat-like faces.

Output Layer

Produces the final probability scores for each category. A softmax function ensures the probabilities sum to 100%, giving you a clear percentage result.

Google's Teachable Machine

We trained our model using Google's Teachable Machine, a powerful yet accessible platform for creating machine learning models. Here's why we chose this approach:

What is Teachable Machine?

Teachable Machine is a web-based tool created by Google that allows anyone to train machine learning models without writing complex code. It uses transfer learning, which means our model builds upon a pre-trained base model that already understands basic image features.

Benefits of Transfer Learning

  • Faster Training: Instead of learning from scratch, the model starts with knowledge of basic visual features
  • Better Accuracy: The pre-trained base was trained on millions of images, providing a strong foundation
  • Efficient Size: The resulting model is optimized for web browsers, loading quickly without sacrificing accuracy

Our Training Process

We carefully curated our training dataset to ensure diverse, representative examples. The model was trained on images featuring:

  • People of different ages, from children to seniors
  • Diverse ethnicities and backgrounds
  • Various facial expressions and angles
  • Different lighting conditions and image qualities

This comprehensive approach helps ensure our model works well for everyone who takes the test.

TensorFlow.js: AI in Your Browser

Our model runs on TensorFlow.js, Google's machine learning library for JavaScript. This technology enables several key features:

Client-Side Processing

All AI computations happen directly in your web browser. When you load our page, the model is downloaded and runs locally on your device. This means:

  • Complete Privacy: Your photos never leave your device or get uploaded to any server
  • No Account Required: We don't need to identify you to provide results
  • Offline Capable: Once loaded, the test works even without an internet connection
  • Instant Results: No network latency - results appear in milliseconds

WebGL Acceleration

TensorFlow.js automatically uses your device's GPU (graphics processor) through WebGL when available. This hardware acceleration makes the AI analysis much faster than CPU-only processing, ensuring quick results even on mobile devices.

What the AI Looks For

Our model has learned to identify specific facial features that distinguish dog-like from cat-like appearances:

Dog-Like Features

  • Rounder face shape
  • Wider-set eyes
  • Fuller cheeks
  • Softer facial contours
  • Broader nose bridge
  • More prominent smile lines
  • Open, expressive features

Cat-Like Features

  • More angular face shape
  • Almond-shaped eyes
  • Higher cheekbones
  • Defined bone structure
  • Narrower nose bridge
  • More subtle expressions
  • Refined, elegant features

Tips for Best Results

While our AI is designed to work with a variety of photos, following these tips will help ensure the most accurate results:

Good Lighting

Natural, even lighting helps the AI clearly see your facial features. Avoid harsh shadows or backlighting.

Face the Camera

A front-facing photo works best. Extreme angles may affect the accuracy of the analysis.

Clear View

Make sure your face is clearly visible without obstructions like sunglasses, masks, or hair covering your face.

Neutral Expression

While the test works with any expression, a relaxed, neutral face often gives the most consistent results.

High Resolution

Higher quality images provide more detail for the AI to analyze. Avoid blurry or pixelated photos.

Single Subject

For best results, use a photo with only one person. The AI may have difficulty with group photos.
